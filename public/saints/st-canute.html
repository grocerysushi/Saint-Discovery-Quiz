Biography

St. Canute was the King of Denmark in the early 11th century, known for his devout Christian faith. He worked to strengthen the church in Denmark and was eventually martyred as he sought to impose reforms and unify the Christian faith. His dedication to his beliefs made him an enduring figure of veneration in both Denmark and the wider Christian world.

Did you know? St. Canute's remains are kept in the cathedral of Odense, which is a major pilgrimage site in Denmark.
