Biography

St. Sixtus II served as pope during a tumultuous time when Christians faced severe persecution under the Roman Empire. He was known for his courageous defense of the faith and his role in promoting the martyrdom of clergy and laity alike. Ultimately, he was martyred while celebrating the Eucharist, exemplifying his commitment to Christ and the Church.

Did you know? St. Sixtus II was the first pope to be martyred in the Vatican.
