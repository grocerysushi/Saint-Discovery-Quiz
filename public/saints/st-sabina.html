St. Sabina

Biography

St. Sabina was a noble Roman woman who embraced Christianity and was martyred for her faith. She is celebrated for her steadfastness in belief, especially during the persecutions against Christians. Her virtues of courage and faith have inspired many in their spiritual journeys.

Did you know? Did you know? St. Sabina was said to have been suffocated to death under a pile of brushwood, symbolizing the purity and strength of her spirit in the face of persecution.
