Biography

St. John Henry Newman was the most prominent Anglican convert to Catholicism in 19th-century England. A leading intellectual and Oxford professor, his conversion caused a national sensation. He was made a cardinal and wrote extensively on the development of doctrine, the nature of faith, and education.

Did you know? His motto as cardinal was 'Cor ad cor loquitur' (Heart speaks to heart), and his conversion took decades of agonized study and prayer.
