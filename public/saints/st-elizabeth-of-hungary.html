St. Elizabeth of Hungary

Feast Day: November 17
Home Take Quiz All Saints By Virtue By Century Feast Calendar

Biography

Born in 1207, Elizabeth was the daughter of the King of Hungary and chose to live a life of poverty despite her royal status. She devoted herself to serving the sick and the poor, famously founding a hospital and providing for many through her own resources. Her unwavering faith and commitment to charity made her a beloved figure, and she was canonized just four years after her death in 1231.

Did you know? St. Elizabeth is credited with performing miracles, including the transformation of bread into roses to conceal her charitable acts from her husband.
