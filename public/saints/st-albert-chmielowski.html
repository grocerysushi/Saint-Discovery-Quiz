Biography

St. Albert Chmielowski, originally an artist, experienced a profound transformation that led him to abandon his career and dedicate his life to serving the marginalized. He founded the Albertine religious congregation, which focused on caring for the poor and homeless in Poland. His life exemplifies the call to charity and compassion, highlighting the importance of serving others in need.

Did you know? Before becoming a religious figure, Albert was a painter and even exhibited his art in Warsaw.

Patron Saint Of

artists, homeless, and those with mental illnesses
