St. Joan of Arc

Feast Day: May 30
Home Take Quiz All Saints By Virtue By Century Feast Calendar

Biography

St. Joan of Arc, a peasant girl from France, claimed to have received divine visions instructing her to support Charles VII in his claim to the French throne. At just 17, she courageously led troops into battle, dramatically changing the course of the war and securing the coronation of Charles VII. Captured by the English, she was executed for her beliefs, becoming a symbol of faith and courage in the face of oppression.

Did you know? St. Joan of Arc is one of the few saints who was a military leader and is often referred to as the 'Maid of Orléans.'
