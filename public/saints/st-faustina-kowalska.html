Biography

St. Faustina Kowalska, known as the 'Apostle of Divine Mercy,' was a Polish nun whose deep spirituality and commitment to God's mercy inspired millions. Through her diaries, she recorded her encounters with Jesus, promoting the message of divine mercy for all humanity. Canonized by Pope John Paul II in 2000, her legacy continues to influence the faith of many around the world.

Did you know? St. Faustina was the first canonized saint of the Divine Mercy devotion, and her diary is considered a spiritual classic.
