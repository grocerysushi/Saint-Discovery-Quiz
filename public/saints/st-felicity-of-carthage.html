St. Felicity of Carthage

Feast Day: March 7
Home Take Quiz All Saints By Virtue By Century Feast Calendar

Biography

St. Felicity was an enslaved woman who was arrested along with her mistress Perpetua and other catechumens in Carthage. She was eight months pregnant at the time of her arrest and gave birth to a daughter in prison just days before her martyrdom. Her bravery alongside Perpetua has made them inseparable symbols of Christian courage.

Did you know? She feared she would not be martyred with her companions because Roman law forbade the execution of pregnant women, but she gave birth just in time.
