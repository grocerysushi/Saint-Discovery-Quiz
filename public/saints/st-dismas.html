St. Dismas

Feast Day: March 25
Home Take Quiz All Saints By Virtue By Century Feast Calendar

Biography

St. Dismas is known as the penitent thief who, during his crucifixion, expressed sorrow for his sins and asked Jesus to remember him. His faith and repentance earned him the promise of paradise that very day, showing that redemption is possible at any point in life. He serves as a powerful symbol of hope for those struggling with sin and despair.

Did you know? St. Dismas is often considered the first saint to enter heaven due to his act of repentance while on the cross.

Patron Saint Of

prisoners, thieves, funeral directors, death row
