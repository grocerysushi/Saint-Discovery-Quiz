St. Leopold Mandić

Feast Day: May 12

Biography

St. Leopold Mandić was a Croatian Capuchin friar known for his extraordinary dedication to the sacrament of confession, often spending up to 15 hours a day in the confessional. His life was characterized by a deep commitment to forgiveness and reconciliation, which inspired many to seek spiritual guidance through him. Despite facing numerous challenges, he remained humble and devoted to serving others until his death in 1942.

Did you know? St. Leopold was so dedicated to hearing confessions that he often had to be reminded to eat and rest.
