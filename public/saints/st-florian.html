Biography

St. Florian was a Roman officer who became a Christian martyr during the reign of Emperor Diocletian. He is celebrated for his steadfast faith and is often depicted emerging from flames. His legacy endures as a symbol of protection against fire and disaster.

Did you know? Despite his martial background, St. Florian's enduring legacy is one of peace and safety, emphasizing protection from fire rather than warfare.

Patron Saint Of

firefighters, chimney sweeps, soldiers, Poland
