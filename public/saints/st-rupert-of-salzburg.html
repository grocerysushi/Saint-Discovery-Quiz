St. Rupert of Salzburg

Feast Day: March 27

Biography

St. Rupert, the first bishop of Salzburg, is celebrated for his missionary work in the 8th century. He founded the Abbey of St. Peter in Salzburg, which became a center of learning and culture. His influence helped to establish Christianity in the Alpine region, making him a key figure in the spread of the faith in Central Europe.

Did you know? St. Rupert is often depicted holding a salt cellar, reflecting his connection to the salt industry in Salzburg.

Patron Saint Of

Salt miners, Salzburg, Austria
