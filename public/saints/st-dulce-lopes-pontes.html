St. Dulce Lopes Pontes

Biography

St. Dulce Lopes Pontes, known as Irmã Dulce, was a Brazilian Franciscan sister who devoted her life to serving the poorest people of Salvador, Bahia. She transformed an abandoned fish market into a hospital, founded schools, orphanages, and health centers. She was nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize and was canonized by Pope Francis in 2019.

Did you know? She began her charitable work by bringing sick people into the chicken coop of her convent when she had no other space to shelter them.
