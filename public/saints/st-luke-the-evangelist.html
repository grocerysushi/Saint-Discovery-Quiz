Biography

St. Luke the Evangelist was a physician and a companion of St. Paul, known for his deep intellect and artistic flair. He penned the Gospel that emphasizes the mercy of Christ and the role of the Holy Spirit in the early Church. His writings have profoundly influenced Christian theology and practice throughout the centuries.

Did you know? St. Luke is often depicted as an ox or calf in religious art, symbolizing sacrifice and service.

Patron Saint Of

Doctors, artists, surgeons, butchers, notaries
