St. Germaine Cousin

Feast Day: June 15

Biography

St. Germaine Cousin was born in a small village in France, where she faced great hardship from a young age due to her physical disabilities and neglect from her family. Despite her suffering, she led a life of piety and charity, often caring for the sick and the poor in her community. Her profound compassion and unwavering faith led to her being canonized as a saint, with many claiming miracles after invoking her name.

Did you know? St. Germaine is often depicted with a lamb, symbolizing her gentle nature and connection to pastoral life.
