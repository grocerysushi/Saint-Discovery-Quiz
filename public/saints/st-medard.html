Biography

St. Medard was a 6th-century bishop known for his piety and dedication to God. He became famous as a protector against storms and was often sought for his intercessions for good weather. His charitable work and commitment to the spiritual welfare of his people made him a beloved figure in the Church.

Did you know? Did you know? St. Medard is often depicted in art holding a goose, which symbolizes his connection to the weather, as he is said to have been born during a storm.

Patron Saint Of

farmers, the weather, the poor, and those suffering from storms
