St. Josephine Bakhita

Feast Day: February 8

Biography

St. Josephine Bakhita was born in Sudan and kidnapped into slavery as a child. After experiencing extreme hardship and discrimination, she was eventually freed and found refuge in Italy, where she joined the Canossian Daughters of Charity. Her unwavering faith and compassion have made her a symbol of hope and resilience for victims of human trafficking worldwide.

Did you know? St. Josephine Bakhita has been declared the patron saint of Sudan, a testament to her enduring connection to her homeland.

Patron Saint Of

Sudan, human trafficking victims, catechists, African missions, survivors of abuse
