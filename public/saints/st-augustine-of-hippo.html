Biography

St. Augustine of Hippo was a 4th-century theologian whose thoughts fundamentally shaped Christian doctrine. Known for his profound writings, especially the 'Confessions' and 'City of God', he played a pivotal role in the development of Western Christianity. His journey from a life of indulgence to a devout Christian exemplifies the transformative power of faith.

Did you know? He was an avid seeker of truth, even after a youth filled with a hedonistic lifestyle and skepticism about religion.
