St. Camillus de Lellis

Feast Day: July 14

Biography

St. Camillus de Lellis, born in 1550 in Italy, led a life dedicated to the sick and suffered from his own health issues that deepened his compassion. After a spiritual conversion, he founded the Camillians, a religious order committed to healthcare service in hospitals and communities. His legacy continues to inspire modern nursing practices and the ethical treatment of patients.

Did you know? St. Camillus is known for carrying a large cross, symbolizing his devotion to Christ and service to the sick.

Patron Saint Of

nurses, hospitals, sick people, the dying, health care workers
